What Does an Air Ticketing Executive Do?

An Air Ticketing Executive is responsible for managing flight reservations, ticketing, and post-booking services for clients. The role sits at the intersection of customer service, technology, and logistics. Key responsibilities include:

  • Booking and issuing flight tickets for domestic and international travel
  • Using Global Distribution Systems (GDS) such as Amadeus, Galileo, or Sabre
  • Calculating fares, taxes, and applicable service fees
  • Understanding and applying fare rules, conditions, and ticket endorsements
  • Handling cancellations, rebookings, refunds, and schedule changes
  • Advising clients on visa requirements, baggage policies, and travel documentation
  • Coordinating with airlines for group bookings, special fares, and seat allocations
  • Maintaining accurate records of all transactions and client communications

The Top 7 Skills You Need

These are the skills that separate a great Air Ticketing Executive from an average one. Each skill is ranked by importance for freshers entering the field in 2026.

Skill 01 · Critical

GDS Proficiency — Amadeus, Galileo, Sabre

Global Distribution Systems (GDS) are the primary tools of an air ticketing professional. Amadeus is the most widely used GDS worldwide, commanding over 40% of the market share, followed by Sabre and Galileo. Proficiency in at least one GDS is non-negotiable for ticketing roles. These systems handle everything from flight availability checks and fare quotes to ticketing, seat selection, and itinerary modifications.

Many training institutes offer 2–3 month GDS certification programmes. At BTW Group, we provide hands-on GDS training to freshers as part of our onboarding process, but having prior exposure will give you a significant advantage.

Essential (95%)
Skill 02 · Critical

Fare Construction and Ticketing Knowledge

Understanding how airfares are built is a core competency. This includes knowledge of fare basis codes, booking classes, fare rules, minimum/maximum stay requirements, advance purchase conditions, and change/cancellation penalties. A skilled ticketing executive can find the most cost-effective fare for a client while ensuring compliance with airline policies.

IATA\'s fare construction training and the BSP (Billing and Settlement Plan) knowledge are highly valued. Even basic familiarity with how fares are calculated will set you apart from other fresher candidates.

Critical (90%)
Skill 03 · High

Geographical Knowledge and Route Planning

An air ticketing executive must have strong knowledge of world geography — countries, capital cities, major airports, airline hubs, and time zones. This is essential for planning logical routes, understanding connecting flights, calculating total travel time, and advising clients on the best routing options. A client travelling from Mumbai to Toronto, for example, may have routing options via Dubai, London, Frankfurt, or New York — each with different airlines, transit times, and fare implications.

Very Important (85%)
Skill 04 · High

Communication and Customer Service Skills

Air ticketing executives interact with clients daily — over the phone, email, and in person. Clients may be confused about baggage policies, anxious about flight changes, or frustrated with cancellations. Clear, patient, and professional communication is essential. You must be able to explain complex fare rules in simple terms, manage expectations about refunds and changes, and maintain a calm demeanour during peak travel seasons when workloads are high.

Very Important (85%)
Skill 05 · High

Attention to Detail and Accuracy

A single character error in a passenger name, date, or flight number can lead to booking rejections, missed flights, or financial liability. Ticketing executives must double-check every detail — passenger names as per passport, flight timings, fare calculations, tax components, and special service requests (meals, wheelchair assistance, seat preferences). Developing a systematic verification habit is one of the most important traits of a successful ticketing professional.

Very Important (80%)
Skill 06 · Medium

Knowledge of Visa and Travel Documentation

Air ticketing and visa services go hand in hand. Many clients book their flights and apply for visas simultaneously. Knowing basic visa requirements for popular destinations — Schengen, US, UK, Canada, Australia, UAE, Thailand, etc. — helps you advise clients better and anticipate potential travel issues. At BTW Group, our ticketing team works closely with the visa processing team, making cross-functional knowledge a major asset.

Valuable (70%)
Skill 07 · Medium

Digital Literacy and AI Tool Familiarity

Modern ticketing involves working with multiple software platforms — GDS interfaces, airline portals, CRM systems, and accounting software. In 2026, familiarity with AI tools is becoming increasingly relevant. AI can assist with fare comparisons, itinerary optimisation, automated responses to common client queries, and even error-checking ticket details. Being comfortable with technology and open to learning new tools will future-proof your career in this field.

Growing (65%)

Do's and Don'ts for Aspiring Air Ticketing Executives

Do's ✔

  • Invest time in a recognised GDS certification
  • Practice fare calculations regularly
  • Stay updated on airline policies and travel regulations
  • Double-check every ticket before issuing
  • Build a habit of systematic documentation

Don'ts ✘

  • Rely only on theory — hands-on GDS practice is essential
  • Ignore fare rules — they can cost clients (and your employer) money
  • Assume all airlines have the same policies
  • Skip double-checking passenger name and passport details
  • Stop learning — the travel industry evolves constantly

FAQs About Air Ticketing Executive Skills

What does an Air Ticketing Executive do?

An Air Ticketing Executive handles flight bookings, ticketing, fare calculations, cancellations, and customer queries related to air travel. They work with Global Distribution Systems (GDS) like Amadeus, Galileo, or Sabre to issue and manage tickets for domestic and international flights.

Do I need a degree to become an Air Ticketing Executive?

A degree in travel, tourism, or hospitality is helpful but not always required. Many employers, including BTW Group, provide on-the-job training. GDS certification and IATA Foundation diplomas can significantly improve your chances of getting hired.

What is the salary of an Air Ticketing Executive in India?

Entry-level Air Ticketing Executives in India earn between INR 15,000 to INR 25,000 per month. With 2–3 years of experience and GDS expertise, salaries can rise to INR 30,000 to INR 45,000 per month. International roles and airlines pay higher.

Which GDS system is best for air ticketing jobs?

Amadeus is the most widely used GDS globally, followed by Galileo and Sabre. For the Indian market, Amadeus and Galileo are most in demand. Learning at least one GDS thoroughly is essential for a career in air ticketing.

Can a fresher get an air ticketing job without experience?

Yes. Many travel agencies and visa consultancies, including BTW Group, hire freshers for air ticketing roles and provide comprehensive training. A GDS certification, good communication skills, and basic geography knowledge will give you an advantage.

What is the career growth for an Air Ticketing Executive?

An Air Ticketing Executive can progress to Senior Ticketing Executive, Team Lead, Ticketing Manager, Operations Manager, or even move into airline roles. With additional certifications, professionals can also specialise in corporate travel management or cruise ticketing.
