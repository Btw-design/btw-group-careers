What Hiring Managers Are Really Asking

Before you craft your answer, understand what the interviewer truly wants to know. When they ask "Why should we hire you?", they are evaluating three things:

  • Self-awareness: Do you know your own strengths? Can you articulate what you bring to the table?
  • Fit: Have you understood the job description? Can you connect your skills to what the role demands?
  • Motivation: Do you genuinely want this job, or are you applying to every opening you find?

According to a 2025 LinkedIn Talent Solutions report, 78% of hiring managers consider the answer to this question a major factor in their decision. And a study by Indeed found that candidates who prepare specific, relevant examples are 40% more likely to progress to the next round.

The 3-Part Answer Framework

The best answers follow a simple three-part structure. We call it the Strength-Relevance-Enthusiasm (SRE) Framework. Use it to organise your thoughts every time:

S
Strength
State 1–2 specific skills or qualities you bring. Anchor them with a brief example if possible.
R
Relevance
Connect your strengths to the job description. Show you understand what the role needs.
E
Enthusiasm
Express genuine interest in the role, the company, and your willingness to learn and grow.

Sample Answers for Freshers

Here are sample answers using the SRE Framework for different types of fresher candidates. Adapt these to your own background and the specific job you are applying for.

For Communication-Focused Roles

Visa Executive, Customer Service, Sales

"You should hire me because I bring strong communication skills and a genuine interest in helping people. During my college project on travel services, I coordinated with 20+ classmates, managed group schedules, and ensured everyone stayed informed — which taught me how to handle inquiries with patience and clarity. This role requires daily client interaction and document accuracy, and I am confident my attention to detail and people skills will help me serve your clients effectively. I am also excited to learn about visa and immigration processes and grow within BTW Group."

Breakdown: Strength (communication + project example) → Relevance (client interaction, accuracy) → Enthusiasm (willingness to learn, long-term commitment)
For Administrative / Operations Roles

Office Assistant, Visa Operation Associate

"I am organised, detail-oriented, and comfortable working with data and documents. In my internship at a local travel agency, I managed filing systems, data entry, and appointment scheduling — and I reduced document retrieval time by organising files digitally. Your job requires someone who can handle multiple applications accurately and meet deadlines without stress. I believe my organisational habits and discipline make me a strong fit. I am eager to bring this structured approach to BTW Group and learn from your experienced team."

Breakdown: Strength (organisation, detail-orientation + internship example) → Relevance (accuracy, deadlines) → Enthusiasm (structured approach, desire to learn)
For Digital / Marketing Roles

Digital Marketing Intern, Marketing Intern

"I have a strong foundation in digital marketing — I completed Google's Digital Marketing certification and managed social media for my college's annual fest, which reached 5,000+ students. I am comfortable with content creation, analytics tools, and trend research. Your marketing intern role needs someone who can create fresh content and understand audience behaviour, and my hands-on experience with social media management directly aligns with that. I am excited to apply my digital skills to real-world campaigns and grow as a marketing professional at BTW Group."

Breakdown: Strength (digital skills, certification + college project) → Relevance (content, audience understanding) → Enthusiasm (excitement for real-world work)
For General Fresher (No Prior Experience)

Any Entry-Level Role

"What I lack in professional experience, I make up for in my willingness to learn and my strong work ethic. During college, I was known as someone who picked up new tools quickly — I taught myself Excel and basic data analysis in two weeks for a group project. I have read about the visa services industry and understand that accuracy and client trust are critical. I am ready to put in the effort to learn every aspect of the role, follow processes carefully, and contribute to your team from day one."

Breakdown: Strength (learnability, work ethic + self-taught example) → Relevance (accuracy, client trust) → Enthusiasm (readiness to learn, contribution mindset)

Do's and Don'ts

Do's ✔

  • Research the company and role before the interview
  • Customise your answer for each job application
  • Use specific examples from college, internships, or projects
  • Keep your answer under 60 seconds
  • Show confidence through your tone and body language
  • Practice aloud until it sounds natural

Don'ts ✘

  • Start with "I am a fresher" or "I have no experience"
  • Give generic answers that could apply to any role
  • Memorise a script word for word
  • Exaggerate your skills or make false claims
  • Speak negatively about previous experiences
  • Ramble beyond 90 seconds

How to Prepare Before the Interview

Great answers are built before the interview, not during it. Use these five preparation steps to walk in confident and ready:

1
Analyse the Job Description
Highlight 2–3 key requirements. These are the skills your answer must connect to.
2
List Your Strengths
Write down 5 skills or qualities. Pick the 2 most relevant to the job.
3
Prepare an Example
For each strength, think of one real situation where you used it.
4
Write & Refine
Draft a 5–6 sentence answer using the SRE Framework. Edit until concise.
5
Practice Aloud
Record yourself, time it, and refine until it flows naturally.

Pro Tip: The 5-Sentence Rule

If your answer fits in 5 sentences, it is probably perfect. 1 sentence for your strength, 1 for your example, 1 connecting to the role, 1 showing enthusiasm, and 1 wrapping it up. Practice until it takes about 50–60 seconds to deliver.

Common Mistakes Freshers Make

We have interviewed hundreds of freshers at BTW Group, and these are the most common mistakes we see when candidates answer this question. Avoid them to stand out:

  • Being too generic: "I am hardworking and sincere" — every candidate says this. Attach each quality to a real example.
  • Not researching the company: When you mention something specific about BTW Group (like our 15 years in visa services or our 3 office locations), it shows genuine interest.
  • Being too long: Answers that stretch beyond 90 seconds lose the interviewer's attention. Be crisp.
  • Sound over-rehearsed: If it sounds like a memorised speech, it feels insincere. Practise for structure, not for exact words.
  • Focusing on what you want: "I want this job because I need experience" — instead, focus on what you can give to the company, not what you can get.

FAQs About Answering "Why Should We Hire You?"

What does "Why should we hire you?" actually mean?

Hiring managers ask this to understand what makes you unique, how your skills align with the role, and what value you can bring to the company. It is your chance to summarise your strengths and fit for the position.

How should a fresher answer "Why should we hire you?"

A fresher should use the 3-part SRE Framework: (1) State your relevant skills and strengths with an example, (2) Connect them to the job requirements, and (3) Express your enthusiasm and willingness to learn. Keep it concise, specific, and honest.

Can I say "I am a fresher" as part of my answer?

Avoid starting with "I am a fresher" or "I have no experience." Instead, focus on what you do have — relevant skills, projects, certifications, and your eagerness to learn. Frame your freshness as adaptability rather than inexperience.

How long should my answer be?

Aim for 45 to 60 seconds. Your answer should be 4 to 6 sentences — long enough to make your point but short enough to keep the interviewer engaged. Practice delivering it naturally without rushing.

What skills should freshers highlight in this answer?

Freshers should highlight communication skills, willingness to learn, attention to detail, computer proficiency, teamwork, problem-solving abilities, and any relevant coursework or certifications. Customise the skills based on the specific job role.

Should I memorise my answer word for word?

No. Memorising a script can make you sound robotic. Instead, remember the key points you want to cover and practise speaking them naturally. Use different words each time while keeping the core message consistent.
