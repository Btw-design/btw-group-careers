Why the Visa Industry Offers Strong Career Growth

The visa and travel services industry in India has experienced consistent growth over the past decade. With increasing outbound travel, student mobility, and business immigration, the demand for skilled visa professionals continues to rise. According to the Ministry of Tourism, Indian outbound travel is projected to reach over 28 million departures by 2027, creating a sustained need for visa processing expertise.

This growth creates a natural career ladder: as companies expand their visa operations, they need experienced professionals to manage teams, ensure quality, and handle complex cases. This is where your growth opportunity lies.

The Career Progression Path

Here is the typical career progression for a Visa Executive at BTW Group, from entry-level to leadership:

L1

Visa Executive (Entry Level)

Your starting point. Learn visa regulations, documentation, application processing, and client communication. Build foundational knowledge across multiple visa categories.

0–12 Months INR 15K–22K/Month Thane / Mumbai
L2

Senior Visa Executive

Handle complex visa cases, mentor new joiners, manage client escalations, and assist with quality checks. You become the go-to person for difficult applications.

12–24 Months INR 22K–28K/Month Thane / Mumbai
L3

Visa Team Leader

Oversee a team of 4–8 Visa Executives. Allocate work, review applications for accuracy, provide coaching, handle escalations, and report on team KPIs.

24–36 Months INR 25K–35K/Month Thane / Mumbai
L4

Operations Manager

Manage multiple teams, drive process improvements, set SLAs, handle strategic client relationships, and contribute to business development and operational planning.

36–60 Months INR 35K–50K+/Month Thane / Mumbai

Typical Timeline: From Executive to Team Leader

Months 0–6: Learning & Settling In

Complete training, learn visa categories, build basic processing speed, and establish a track record of accuracy. Focus on absorbing as much as possible.

Months 6–12: Becoming Reliable

Process applications independently, handle standard client queries, achieve target processing volumes, and demonstrate consistency. Aim for zero-error days.

Months 12–18: Taking on More

Start handling complex applications, assist with training new joiners, volunteer for additional responsibilities, and build cross-functional knowledge.

Months 18–24: Senior Executive

Formally step into a Senior Visa Executive role. Mentor juniors, handle escalations, perform quality checks, and demonstrate leadership behaviour daily.

Months 24–36: Team Leader

Promoted to Team Leader. Manage your own team, own KPIs, drive performance, and become the bridge between the operations and management teams.

What BTW Group Looks for in a Future Team Leader

When considering internal promotions to Team Leader, BTW Group evaluates the following qualities:

  • Consistent performance: Sustained accuracy and volume output over at least 18 months
  • Initiative: Willingness to take on extra responsibilities, help others, and go beyond the job description
  • Problem-solving ability: Can handle difficult cases and client escalations without constant supervision
  • Communication skills: Clear written and verbal communication, both with clients and team members
  • Team spirit: Collaborates well, earns respect from peers, and demonstrates leadership potential naturally
  • Learning attitude: Open to feedback, quick to learn new processes, and actively seeks self-improvement

Tips to Accelerate Your Growth

  1. Master the basics first. Before you can lead, you must be excellent at the core job. Focus on accuracy and speed in your first year.
  2. Volunteer for training. Offer to help train new joiners. Teaching others is the best way to deepen your own knowledge.
  3. Learn beyond your role. Understand how the entire visa process works — from client acquisition to application submission to post-visa services.
  4. Ask for feedback regularly. Don't wait for annual reviews. Ask your manager monthly: "What can I do better to grow in this role?"
  5. Document your wins. Keep a record of your achievements — high accuracy rates, positive client feedback, successful escalations — to present during promotion reviews.
  6. Build cross-functional skills. Learn about travel insurance, ticketing, and customer service. The more you know about the broader business, the more valuable you become.
  7. Show leadership every day. You don't need a title to lead. Take ownership, help teammates, and act like a leader before you are one.

FAQs About Visa Executive Career Growth

How long does it take to go from Visa Executive to Team Leader at BTW Group?

Typically, it takes 2–3 years of consistent performance to move from a Visa Executive role to a Team Leader position at BTW Group. High-performing executives may be considered for senior roles in as little as 18–24 months.

What skills do I need to become a Visa Team Leader?

Key skills for a Visa Team Leader include: in-depth knowledge of visa regulations across multiple countries, team management and delegation, quality assurance and audit experience, client relationship management, training and mentoring ability, and strong problem-solving skills under pressure.

What is the salary range for a Visa Team Leader at BTW Group?

At BTW Group, a Visa Team Leader earns between INR 25,000 and INR 35,000 per month, depending on experience, performance, and team size. This represents a significant jump from the starting Visa Executive salary of INR 15,000–INR 22,000.

Does BTW Group provide training for career growth?

Yes. BTW Group has a strong internal promotion culture and provides regular training programs, cross-functional exposure, and mentorship opportunities. Visa Executives are encouraged to upskill through on-the-job training, process documentation, and leadership development.

Can I join BTW Group as a fresher and become a Team Leader?

Absolutely. Many of our current Team Leaders and managers started their careers as fresher Visa Executives at BTW Group. We promote from within and value loyalty, consistent performance, and a willingness to learn.

What are the responsibilities of a Visa Team Leader?

A Visa Team Leader oversees a team of 4–8 Visa Executives, allocates workloads, reviews applications for accuracy and compliance, provides coaching and feedback, handles escalations, reports to management on team KPIs, and ensures service level agreements (SLAs) are met.
