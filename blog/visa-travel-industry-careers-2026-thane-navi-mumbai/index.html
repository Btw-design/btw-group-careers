Why the Visa and Travel Industry Is Booming in 2026

Several factors are driving unprecedented growth in India's visa and travel sector:

  • Post-pandemic travel surge: International tourist arrivals in India are expected to exceed pre-2019 levels by 15% in 2026, according to the Ministry of Tourism. Outbound travel from India is also projected to grow at a compound annual rate of 11% through 2028.
  • Expanding overseas education: Over 1.5 million Indian students are studying abroad in 2026, with top destinations including Canada, the United States, Australia, the United Kingdom, Germany, and Ireland. Each student visa application requires meticulous documentation and expert guidance.
  • New visa policies and routes: Countries like Thailand, Sri Lanka, Malaysia, and Kenya have introduced visa-on-arrival or e-visa facilities for Indian travellers, while Schengen and UK visa processes continue to evolve. This creates ongoing demand for knowledgeable visa consultants.
  • Corporate travel recovery: Business travel has rebounded strongly, with Indian companies sending employees abroad for meetings, conferences, and project assignments. Corporate visa services are a growing segment.
  • Thane and Navi Mumbai as emerging hubs: These twin cities in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region have become thriving centres for travel and visa businesses, driven by their excellent connectivity, affordable real estate, and large talent pool of educated freshers.

Top Career Opportunities in Visa and Travel Services in 2026

The visa and travel industry offers a wide range of career paths suited to different interests and aptitudes. Here are the most in-demand roles in Thane and Navi Mumbai:

1. Visa Executive / Visa Processing Associate

Visa executives are the backbone of any visa service organisation. They handle the end-to-end visa application process — from client consultation and document verification to form filling, embassy liaison, and status tracking. This role is ideal for freshers who are detail-oriented, organised, and enjoy helping people navigate complex processes.

2. Air Ticketing Executive

Air ticketing executives specialise in booking flights, managing reservations, handling cancellations and reissues, and providing travel advice to clients. Knowledge of Global Distribution Systems (GDS) such as Amadeus, Galileo, or Sabre is a key skill, though many employers — including BTW Group — provide GDS training to freshers.

3. Travel Consultant

Travel consultants provide end-to-end travel planning services, including itinerary design, flight and hotel bookings, travel insurance, and visa assistance. This role requires strong customer service skills, destination knowledge, and the ability to tailor travel experiences to client preferences.

4. Customer Service Executive (Travel)

Customer service executives in the travel industry handle inbound inquiries from clients via phone, email, and chat. They provide information about visa requirements, travel packages, document checklists, and application status. This role is a great entry point into the industry and offers exposure to all aspects of travel services.

5. Documentation Specialist

Documentation specialists focus on the verification, organisation, and quality control of visa and travel documents. They ensure that every application meets the specific requirements of the destination country, reducing the risk of rejections and delays. This role requires a sharp eye for detail and excellent organisational skills.

Essential Skills for a Career in Visa and Travel Services

Unlike many technical fields, the visa and travel industry does not require a specialised degree. What matters most is the right combination of soft skills and a willingness to learn. Here are the skills that will help you succeed:

1. Communication Skills

You will interact with clients, embassies, airlines, and colleagues daily. Clear, professional, and empathetic communication — both verbal and written — is essential for building trust and ensuring smooth processes.

2. Attention to Detail

Visa applications and travel bookings involve numerous documents, dates, and regulations. A single error can cause delays, rejections, or financial loss. Accuracy is one of the most valued traits in this industry.

3. Customer Service Mindset

Many clients approach visa and travel services with a mix of excitement and anxiety. Patience, empathy, and a genuine desire to help create positive experiences that drive repeat business and referrals.

4. Basic Computer & Internet Proficiency

You will work with online application portals, email, MS Office (especially Excel and Word), and internal CRM systems. Basic digital literacy is a prerequisite for most roles.

5. Adaptability & Learning Agility

Visa policies, travel regulations, and airline rules change frequently. The ability to quickly learn new procedures and adapt to changing requirements is highly valued by employers.

6. Organisational Skills

Managing multiple client files simultaneously, tracking deadlines, and prioritising tasks are part of daily life in visa and travel services. Good organisational skills help you stay on top of your workload.

Why Thane and Navi Mumbai Are Great Locations for Travel Careers

Thane and Navi Mumbai have emerged as preferred destinations for visa and travel businesses. Here is why:

  • Strategic location: Close to Mumbai's international airport and business districts, with excellent railway and road connectivity, making them accessible for both clients and employees.
  • Growing population: Thane's population is projected to exceed 2.5 million by 2026, while Navi Mumbai continues to expand rapidly, creating a large local market for travel and visa services.
  • Affordable living: Compared to Mumbai, the cost of living in Thane and Navi Mumbai is significantly lower, making them attractive for freshers starting their careers.
  • Educational institutions: Both cities are home to numerous colleges and universities, producing a steady stream of qualified graduates seeking entry-level employment.
  • Established business ecosystem: From IT parks in Airoli and Ghansoli to commercial hubs in Vashi, Thane West, and Navi Mumbai, the region has a mature business infrastructure.
